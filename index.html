About Accreditation

Accreditation in the Philippines has emerged as a method of self-regulation and peer review adopted by the educational institutions. Educational programs get accredited, but the participation is voluntary. The main purpose of accreditation is quality assurance. An accredited status indicates that an institution and program have been evaluated and found to meet the standards set by the accreditation agency.

Accreditation is a quality assurance process in which an institution's programs, services, and operations are examined by a third-party accrediting agency to determine if applicable standards are met. If standards are met, accredited status is granted by the agency.

AACCUP is currently using a 10-area evaluation instrument. However, the accreditors' recommendations are very vital to uplift the status of each program and to enhance its quality. We are counting on your support in ensuring that all areas will be improved in accordance with the recommendations of the accreditors.
